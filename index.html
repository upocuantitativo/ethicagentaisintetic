Research Methodology

Mixed-methods approach: OSINT + NLP + Manual Verification

Research Objective

This study investigates the emergence of synthetic companies — legal entities created or operated primarily by AI agents with minimal or no human involvement. We aim to: (1) develop a taxonomy of AI-enabled organizational forms, (2) build a detection methodology to identify them, (3) construct a validated international sample, and (4) analyze the ethical, governance, and accountability implications for a high-impact academic article.

Context: Sam Altman (OpenAI) predicted "one-person billion-dollar companies" powered by AI agents. Dario Amodei (Anthropic) expects this by 2026. Meanwhile, cases like HurumoAI (a Wired experiment with fully AI employees), Dictador's robot CEO "Mika", and NetDragon's AI CEO "Tang Yu" show this is already happening. We study the full spectrum: from legitimate agentic organizations to fully synthetic (potentially fraudulent) entities.

Synthetic Score — Detection Algorithm

Each entity receives a composite score (0–100) based on 8 weighted signals. Higher scores indicate greater likelihood of being synthetic:

Score Interpretation

  • 0–30: Likely a real company with legitimate operations. AI is a tool, not the operator.
  • 31–50: Agentic organization — heavy AI use but clear human presence. Needs review.
  • 51–70: Ambiguous — could be heavily automated or partially synthetic. Requires deep verification (Phase 4).
  • 71–85: High probability of synthetic entity. Multiple red flags detected. Priority for human verification.
  • 86–100: Almost certainly synthetic or a shell. AI-generated content, no employees, phantom address, no financial records.

8 Detection Signals

  • S1 (20%): No verified employees (LinkedIn, Glassdoor cross-check)
  • S2 (15%): AI-generated web content (GLTR/GPTZero score)
  • S3 (15%): Virtual/fake registered address
  • S4 (15%): Missing financial records or zero filings
  • S5 (10%): Domain age vs. incorporation date mismatch
  • S6 (10%): Web traffic anomalies (bot ratio, fake sources)
  • S7 (10%): No real product or service delivered
  • S8 (5%): AI references in corporate identity ("AI CEO")

Data Reliability Index (DRI) — How We Measure Confidence

The DRI is a meta-indicator that measures how much we can trust the data collected. It is calculated from 4 components:

Source Diversity (25%)

  • How many independent sources confirm a data point?
  • Single source = low; 3+ sources = high
  • Penalizes over-reliance on one platform

Cross-Validation Rate (25%)

  • % of entities validated by 2+ methods
  • Registry + OSINT + contact = triple validation
  • Target: >60% of sample

Verification Depth (25%)

  • How many of the 7 verification steps completed per entity?
  • 1-2 steps = shallow; 5+ = deep
  • Deeper = more reliable classification

Sample Coverage (25%)

  • Geographic + sector representativeness
  • Min. 20 countries, 5 entity types
  • Avoids US/UK bias in results

Decision Rule: An entity is classified as "Confirmed Synthetic" only if it fails 3 or more verification steps (B–E). If it fails 1–2 steps, it is classified as "Indeterminate" and flagged for additional investigation. If it passes all steps, it is "Dismissed" as a legitimate (though potentially agentic) organization. Every classification decision must be documented with evidence and reasoning.

Purposive Sampling

Snowball / Referral

  • Each confirmed case leads to related entities (partners, competitors, copycats)
  • DAO networks often reference each other
  • Expert interviews may reveal unpublished cases

    Detection Signals Matrix

    LLM-Generated Content

    Homogeneous, repetitive text on websites. Use GLTR, GPTZero, or similar detectors. Check metadata for AI authoring tools.

    Domain & WHOIS Analysis

    Recently registered domains, privacy-protected WHOIS, generic registrars. Cross-reference with incorporation dates.

    Traffic Anomalies

    Automated traffic spikes, unusual geographic distribution, no organic search presence, zero backlinks from real sites.

    No Real Employees

    Zero LinkedIn employees, no photos of team, fake headshots (StyleGAN), no glassdoor reviews, generic "about us" pages.

    24/7 Automated Responses

    Instant replies at all hours, chatbot-only communication, no phone line, template-based email responses.

    Virtual/Phantom Office

    Registered at mailbox services, coworking generic addresses, or empty locations. Verify via Street View and site visits.

    Missing Legal Compliance

    No tax filings, zero reported revenue, missing statutory accounts, no named directors (or offshore shell directors).

    AI in Corporate Identity

    References to "AI CEO", "robot director", or "autonomous management" in press releases or corporate docs (e.g., Dictador's Mika).

    Shell Company Patterns

    Circular ownership, nominee shareholders, layered offshore entities with no clear beneficial owner.
